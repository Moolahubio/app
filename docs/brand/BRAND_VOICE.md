# MoolaHub Brand Voice (quick reference)

Keep MoolaHub sounding like a trustworthy fintech written by people. Use this for every new string.

## Who we sound like
A sharp, trustworthy friend who works in finance: explains money plainly, never hypes, never talks down, genuinely glad when you reach a goal.

## Four attributes
- **Clear** — plain, concrete, jargon-light. Not vague, not dumbed-down.
- **Trustworthy** — calm and precise about money. Not salesy or emoji-happy.
- **Warm** — encouraging and human. Not chirpy or childish.
- **Confident** — direct, verb-first. Not hedging or padded.

## Hard rules
1. **Sentence case** everywhere — labels, headings, buttons, toasts. Never Title Case.
2. **Em-dash diet** — at most one per screen; prefer a period or comma. (Em-dash-as-connector is the #1 "AI-written" tell.)
3. **No emoji in money or transactional copy.** Default to none for a banking feel.
4. **Exclamation marks are rare** and never on a money movement.
5. **Verb-first, consistent CTAs:** "Create goal", "Start circle", "Add money", "Send", "Withdraw". One verb per action across the app.
6. **Cut filler:** "Simply", "Let's…", "one X at a time", "in minutes" (unless literally true).
7. **No unsubstantiated money claims.** Talk about *saving* and *reaching goals*, not *growing money* or returns, unless Legal-approved and disclosed.
8. **Currency/numbers consistent:** `$1,000`, `100 USDC`, `2%`. Real ellipsis character `…`, not `...`.

## Terminology (use consistently)
sign in / sign up (verbs) · wallet · circle (define "Susu" once) · goal · available balance · contribution · round · escrow/vault (with a one-line plain explainer the first time).

## Microcopy patterns
- **Empty state:** what's here + the one action. "No goals yet. Create your first one."
- **Confirmation (money):** specific + past tense, calm. "$980 from {circle} is now in your available balance."
- **Error:** blame-free + next step. "We couldn't sign you in. Please try again."
- **Loading:** "Loading your goals…" (real ellipsis).

## Quick test for any string
Read it aloud. If it sounds like a press release, a hype ad, or a chatbot — rewrite it plainer and calmer. If a bank wouldn't put an emoji or an exclamation mark there, don't.
