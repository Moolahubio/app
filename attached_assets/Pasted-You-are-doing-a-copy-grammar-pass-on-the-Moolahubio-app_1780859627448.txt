The voice
A trustworthy fintech written by people: clear, calm, warm but not chirpy, confident. Explain money plainly; never hype; be genuinely glad when someone reaches a goal.
Hard rules (apply to every string)

Sentence case everywhere — labels, headings, buttons, toasts, dialog titles. Never Title Case. ("Goal name", not "Goal Name".)
No em-dash as a connector. Use a period or comma. (The em-dash-as-connector is the #1 AI tell.) A lone "—" as an empty-value placeholder is fine.
No emoji in money or transactional copy — especially notify()/toast titles and bodies, and emails. (A celebratory emoji on a share image is the only allowed exception.)
Exclamation marks are rare and never on a money movement.
Verb-first, consistent CTAs: "Create goal", "Start a circle", "Add money", "Send", "Withdraw". One verb per action across the whole app.
Real ellipsis: "Loading your goals…" with the … character, never "Loading...".
No filler: "Simply", "Let's…", "one X at a time", "in minutes" (unless literally true), "chase", "rally".
No growth/return claims: never "grow your money", "grow together", or imply returns. Talk about saving and reaching goals. Don't show a bare APY/yield number without a "variable" + disclosure (flag for me if you find one).
No false claims: don't say contracts are "audited" unless they've had an external audit.
Currency/numbers consistent: $1,000, 100 USDC, 2%.

Microcopy patterns

Empty state: what's here + the one action. "No goals yet. Create your first one."
Money confirmation: specific + past tense, calm. "$980 from {circle} is now in your available balance."
Error: blame-free + next step, generic about internals. "We couldn't sign you in. Please try again."
Loading: "Loading your goals…"
